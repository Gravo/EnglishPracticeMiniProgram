# Middle East History Learning Framework

## Why the US Cares About the Middle East

### 1. Oil Resources (Core Reason)
- Middle East holds about 60% of world's oil reserves
- 1973 oil crisis taught US the importance of energy security
- Petrodollar system linked to US dollar dominance

### 2. Geopolitics
- Middle East connects three continents: Asia, Europe, Africa
- Suez Canal is crucial global shipping route
- Control Middle East = control the world island's hub

### 3. Israel Alliance
- US most important strategic ally in Middle East
- Jewish influence in US politics and economy
- Democratic values "outpost"

### 4. War on Terror
- After 9/11, Middle East became main battlefield
- Prevent extremism spread
- Protect US homeland security

## Major Wars and Conflicts

### 1. 1948 Arab-Israeli War
- Cause: Israel's founding, Arab opposition
- Result: Israel victory, Palestinian refugee crisis
- Impact: Beginning of Arab-Israeli conflict

### 2. 1956 Suez Crisis
- Cause: Egypt nationalized Suez Canal
- Parties: UK-France-Israel vs Egypt
- Result: US-USSR pressure, UK-France withdrawal
- Impact: End of colonial system, US-USSR rise

### 3. 1967 Six-Day War
- Cause: Arab countries mobilized armies
- Result: Israel captured Sinai, Gaza, West Bank, Golan Heights in 6 days
- Impact: Israel territory expanded, Palestinian issue complicated

### 4. 1973 Yom Kippur War
- Cause: Egypt-Syria wanted to reclaim territory
- Result: Early Arab victory, then Israel counterattack
- Impact: Oil crisis (OPEC embargo), US began leading peace process

### 5. 1979 Iranian Revolution
- Cause: Pahlavi corruption, religious forces rose
- Result: Khomeini established Islamic Republic
- Impact: US embassy hostage crisis, US-Iran relations broken

### 6. 1980-1988 Iran-Iraq War
- Parties: Iraq vs Iran
- Cause: Territory disputes, religious conflicts, Arab vs Persian
- Result: Both sides lost, millions died
- Impact: Saddam rose, paved way for Gulf War

### 7. 1990-1991 Gulf War
- Cause: Iraq invaded Kuwait
- Result: US-led coalition victory
- Impact: US military presence normalized in Middle East

### 8. 2003-2011 Iraq War
- Cause: US claimed WMD (not found)
- Result: Saddam fell, Iraq chaos
- Impact: Shia power, Iran influence expanded, ISIS rose

### 9. 2010-2012 Arab Spring
- Scope: Tunisia, Egypt, Libya, Syria
- Results:
  - Tunisia: Democratic transition (relatively successful)
  - Egypt: Military regained power
  - Libya: Gaddafi fell, country divided
  - Syria: Civil war erupted, continues today

### 10. 2011-present Syrian Civil War
- Parties:
  - Government forces (Russia, Iran supported)
  - Opposition (US, Turkey, Gulf states supported)
  - Kurdish forces
  - ISIS (defeated but not eradicated)
- Impact: Millions displaced, European refugee crisis

## Current Complex Environment Causes

### Religious Factors
- Sunni vs Shia: Saudi vs Iran proxy war
- Extremism: Al-Qaeda, ISIS rise
- Religion-politics mix: Iran theocracy, Saudi Wahhabism

### Ethnic Factors
- Arabs, Persians, Turks, Kurds competition
- Kurdish independence movement

### Great Power Competition
- US: Maintain oil interests, Israel security, contain Iran
- Russia: Return to Middle East, support Syria, counter NATO
- China: Belt and Road, energy imports, balanced diplomacy
- EU: Energy dependence, refugee issues, limited intervention
